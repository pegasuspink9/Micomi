--What Is an HTML Element?:--

• An *HTML Element* is the basic building block of a webpage. It is like a Lego piece for your site!
• It is made of three parts: an *Opening Tag*, the *Content* (the text you want people to see), and a *Closing Tag*.
• Think of it like a room: the opening tag is the front door, the content is the furniture inside, and the closing tag is the back door!

Example:
<p>This is my paragraph content!</p>
<h1>This is my big heading!</h1>

--Elements vs. Tags: What is the Difference?:--

• It is easy to get confused between elements and tags, so let's think about a *sandwich*!
• An *HTML Tag* is just the opening `<p>` or closing `</p>`. They are the slices of bread.
• An *HTML Element* is the *ENTIRE* thing together: the top bread (opening tag), the yummy filling (content), and the bottom bread (closing tag).

Example:
<div>I am a complete HTML element. My tags are the bread!</div>

--Nested Elements: Boxes Inside Boxes:--

• HTML lets you put elements *inside* other elements! This is called *Nesting*.
• Think of it like a big box that holds smaller boxes inside it. The outside element is the *parent*, and the inside element is the *child*.
• *Important Rule:* The tag you open *last* must be closed *first*! 

Example:
<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
<head>
   <title>Nested Magic</title>
</head>
<body>
   <h1>This is an <i>italic</i> word in a heading!</h1>
   <p>This is an <u>underlined</u> word in a paragraph.</p>
</body>
</html>

End of example

--Special Empty Elements: The Rule Breakers!:--

• Most elements need an opening and closing tag, but some are called *Empty (Void) Elements*.
• They do not have text filling inside, so they *do not need a closing tag*!
• The *Line Break Tag* `<br>` moves text to the next line, and the *Horizontal Rule Tag* `<hr>` draws a line across the screen. They stand all alone!

Example:
<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
<body>
   <p>Look at this line.<br>Now I am on a brand new line!</p>
   <hr>
   <p>I am separated by a cool straight line.</p>
</body>
</html>

End of example

--HTML Attributes: Giving Tags Superpowers:--

• *Attributes* are secret instructions you put *inside the opening tag* to give it special powers.
• They tell the tag extra details about what to do.
• For example, when you use an empty image tag `<img>`, you need to tell the browser *which* picture to show by using the `src` (source) attribute!

Example:
<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
<body>
   <p>Here is a picture of a logo:</p>
   <img src="logo.jpg" alt="A cool logo">
</body>
</html>

--HTML Golden Rules: Closing Tags and Lowercase:--

• *Rule 1: Always close your tags!* If you forget to close a `<p>` tag, the browser might get confused and your webpage might look messy.
• *Rule 2: Use lowercase letters!* Browsers are smart and *can* read uppercase like `<P>`, but it is not good practice. Always use lowercase letters like `<p>` so your code looks clean and professional!

Example:
<!DOCTYPE html>
<html>
<body>
   <p>This is the perfect way to write a tag! All lowercase and safely closed.</p>
   <P>THIS WORKS BUT IT IS SHOUTING! Try not to use uppercase letters for tags.</P>
</body>
</html>

End of example
